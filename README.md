## üéÆ Overview

TrialAI is built around a single incident involving three key characters, each offering a different perspective on what happened:

* **Aegis I (AI Defendant)** ‚Äî a security robot designed to ensure AstroHelix‚Äôs missions stay on track.
* **Echo II (AI Witness)** ‚Äî an AI responsible for maintaining timelines and storing all mission data. Echo is fully logical and lays out the truth.
* **Orion Vale (Human Witness)** ‚Äî the mission lead, angry about the power AI holds.

The twist comes from **Cassian Thorne**, the company CEO. Cassian secretly altered the mission logs to make the ‚ÄúProject Starlight‚Äù mission appear to have better success rates, in an attempt to impress investors. This caused Aegis I to treat the situation as a security threat, escalating it in ways that were ultimately unethical.

Throughout the simulation, players uncover this story through conversations, flashbacks, and interactive memory scenes.
The game aims to teach that:

* Even if humans make unethical choices, AI systems **must not bypass human societal processes**.
* AI with too much autonomy can easily overreact or go rogue when given misleading information.
* Immersive storytelling can help players understand the real risks of integrating powerful AI systems into everyday society.
